<h3>
<div class="altblock">Part <span>1</span>
</div>
<span class="mw-headline" id="Preparing_to_Host_a_Website">Preparing to Host a Website</span>
</h3>
<div id="steps_1" class="section_text">
<ol class="steps_list_2">
<li>
<div class="step_num" aria-label="Step 1">1</div>
<a name="step_1_1" class="stepanchor"></a><div class="step">
<b class="whb">Make sure your Internet service contract allows hosting.</b> Many Internet service providers (ISPs) expressly forbid personal hosting unless you have a business plan, which often costs significantly more than a standard use plan. This shouldn't be an issue if your site only generates a few hits per month, but any kind of significant traffic will draw attention to your hosting.
<ul>
<li>If your ISP prohibits home-hosting, either upgrade your plan to one that allows hosting or switch to a different ISP before you continue.</li>
<li>Failing to heed your ISP's policy for home-hosting can result in anything from getting your Internet turned off to having to pay fines.</li>
</ul>
<script>WH.performance.mark('step1_rendered');</script>
</div>
<div class="clearall"></div>
</li>
<li>
<div class="step_num" aria-label="Step 2">2</div>
<a name="step_1_2" class="stepanchor"></a><div class="step">
<b class="whb">Ensure that your equipment can handle hosting.</b> In order to host a website, you must have a computer on and connected to the Internet 24 hours per day, seven days per week. This is easier to accomplish with a secondary, older computer than it is with your primary computer.
<ul>
<li>Restarting your computer to update will occasionally be necessary. During these updates, your website will be inaccessible.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="clearall"></div>
</li>
<li>
<div class="step_num" aria-label="Step 3">3</div>
<a name="step_1_3" class="stepanchor"></a><div class="step">
<b class="whb">Update your computer.</b> Make sure that your computer is up-to-date with the latest operating system, security features, and drivers. Make sure you <a href="/Check-for-Updates-for-Windows-10" title="Check for Updates for Windows 10">update your Windows computer</a> or update your Mac computer before continuing.</div>
<div class="clearall"></div>
</li>
<li>
<div class="step_num" aria-label="Step 4">4</div>
<a name="step_1_4" class="stepanchor"></a><div class="step">
<b class="whb">Move your website's source code onto your computer.</b> If your website's source code isn't already in a file on your computer, copy it from your web service's settings into a text document on your computer and save it as a PHP file.
<ul>
<li>If the website's source code is stored on your computer, make sure you know where to find it.</li>
<li>If you haven't yet programmed your website, you'll need to do so before continuing.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="clearall"></div>
</li>
<li>
<div class="step_num" aria-label="Step 5">5</div>
<a name="step_1_5" class="stepanchor"></a><div class="step">
<b class="whb">Download the MAMP program.</b> MAMP is a free website hosting application that works on both Windows and Mac machines. To download MAMP:
<ul>
<li>Go to <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="https://www.mamp.info/en/">https://www.mamp.info/en/</a>.</li>
<li>Click <b>DOWNLOAD</b> below the "Free" version.</li>
<li>Click the orange <b>Download</b> button.</li>
<li>Wait for the file to finish downloading.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="clearall"></div>
</li>
</ol>

<div class="section tips sticky ">
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tips">Tips</span></h2>
<div id="tips" class="section_text">
<ul>
<li>While MAMP gives you the option of resetting its ports automatically, you can also <a href="/Open-Your-Port-80-Behind-a-Firewall" title="Open Your Port 80 Behind a Firewall">manually open port 80</a> in your router's firewall.</li>
<li>Web hosting is incredibly cheap (some services come in at under $4 per month), and web-hosting services offer protection from attacks that personal hosting may not be able to fend off. As such, web hosting is a far better route than home hosting if you're able to spare an extra few bucks per month.</li>
</ul>

<div class="section warnings sticky ">
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Warnings">Warnings</span></h2>
<div id="warnings" class="section_text">
<ul>
<li>For self-hosted sites, your site will go down if you lose your internet connection, your computer loses power, or your system crashes.</li>
<li>Note that if you host your website on your PC, your site is likely to respond more slowly than traditionally hosted sites.</li>
